This Code of Conduct establishes the behavioral standards for participation. It reflects principles found in RFC 7154 (IETF Guidelines for Conduct) and practices used across open source communities.

The goal is a collaborative environment that produces accurate, fair, and trustworthy content.

---

# 1. Core Principles

## 1.1 Integrity and Independence

Participants MUST:

- avoid affiliate links, referral codes, and paid promotions
- disclose conflicts of interest
- avoid manipulating discussions or consensus
- refrain from falsifying identity or using sockpuppet accounts

## 1.2 Accuracy and Intellectual Honesty

Participants SHOULD:

- make evidence-based statements
- distinguish opinion from fact
- be willing to correct errors
- avoid exaggeration or misrepresentation

## 1.3 Respect and Professionalism

As emphasized in RFC 7154, participants MUST:

- critique ideas, not individuals
- avoid hostile or aggressive behavior
- communicate clearly and constructively

---

# 2. Expected Behavior
Participants MUST:

- use respectful and inclusive language
- collaborate in good faith
- consider the impact of their words
- reply to disagreements with clarity and professionalism

---

# 3. Unacceptable Behavior

The following behavior is prohibited:

- harassment, threats, or intimidation
- personal or political attacks
- hateful or discriminatory language
- doxxing or sharing personal data
- trolling, flamebaiting, or provocation
- undisclosed promotional or sponsored content
- deliberate manipulation of consensus or participation metrics

---

# 4. Reporting Violations

Violations may be reported through GitHub or via the moderation contact detailed in **MODERATION.md**.
Reports will be handled confidentially.

---

# 5. Enforcement

The enforcement process is documented in **MODERATION.md** and includes:

- private warnings
- cooling-off periods
- temporary restrictions
- permanent removal for severe misconduct

Moderation actions seek to preserve community integrity and productive collaboration.

---

# 6. Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- GitHub issues
- pull requests
- discussions
- reviews
- all project-affiliated channels
