Thank you for your interest in contributing. This Project relies on transparent, evidence-based collaboration. Participation is governed by the Note Well, the Code of Conduct, and the moderation procedures described in MODERATION.md.

---

# 1. Contribution Principles

Contributions MUST:

- be factual, evidence-based, and clearly reasoned
- avoid affiliate links or promotional content
- respect the Code of Conduct
- follow applicable review standards

Contributors should review RFC 7282's principles of rough consensus, as they influence how decisions are made.

---

# 2. Types of Contributions

You MAY contribute:

- product or service reviews
- factual corrections
- improvements to existing content
- documentation
- UI/UX changes
- bug fixes
- new review categories

You MUST NOT contribute:

- paid or promotional material
- unverifiable claims
- spam or mass-generated content
- personal attacks or commentary unrelated to the product or service

---

# 3. Review Formatting Standards

Reviews SHOULD include:

1. Description of what was evaluated
2. Usage context
3. Strengths and weaknesses
4. Evidence (benchmarks, data, comparisons, sourcing)
5. Clear separation of fact and opinion
6. Disclosure of conflicts of interest

---

# 4. Pull Requests

To submit a PR:

1. Fork the repository
2. Create a new branch
3. Commit focused changes
4. Submit a PR describing:
   - what changed
   - why the change matters
   - supporting evidence

PRs will be evaluated using rough consensus rather than voting.

---

# 5. Issues

Use issues for:

- discussing errors or inaccuracies
- proposing new categories
- requesting clarifications
- raising moderation or governance concerns

Issues MUST remain professional and follow the Code of Conduct.

---

# 6. Conflict of Interest

Contributors MUST disclose:

- employment by the reviewed entity
- receipt of free products or compensation
- financial stake in the product or service

Failure to disclose conflicts may result in moderation action.

---

# 7. Communication Expectations

Participants MUST:

- communicate respectfully
- provide substantiated objections when disagreeing
- collaborate to reach rough consensus
- remain open to revision or correction

These expectations reflect the collaborative principles outlined in RFC 7154.
